Privacy Policy

Antigravity Alliance Inc. — AllyOS & The Kite & Wing Alliance Last Updated: March 29, 2026 Effective Date: March 29, 2026

1. Who We Are

This Privacy Policy explains how Antigravity Alliance Inc. ("we," "us," "our"), a Delaware C-Corp operating from Cape Town, South Africa, collects, uses, shares, and protects your personal information when you use our platforms, websites, mobile applications, and related services (collectively, the "Services").

We operate two distinct services:

  1. AllyOS — A school management SaaS platform enabling booking, student progress tracking, and instructor coordination
  2. The Kite & Wing Alliance — A community platform for kitesurfing, wingfoiling, and windsurfing enthusiasts with session logging, spot journals, leaderboards, and community features

Data Controller: Antigravity Alliance Inc. Registered: Delaware, United States Operating address: Cape Town, South Africa

Privacy Contact: privacy@thekitealliance.com Legal Contact: legal@thekitealliance.com Support Contact: support@thekitealliance.com Data Protection Officer: dpo@thekitealliance.com

2. Information We Collect

2.1 Information You Provide Directly

  • Account Information: Name, email address, password, profile photo, date of birth, country of residence, preferred language, and communication preferences.
  • School & Instructor Profiles (AllyOS): Business name, school location, certifications, instructor qualifications, operating licenses, pricing, availability, and student roster.
  • Rider Profiles (Kite & Wing Alliance): Skill level, preferred spots, equipment, competition history, and profile bio.
  • Booking & Transaction Data: Lesson bookings, session history, payment details (processed by Stripe — we do not store full card numbers), billing address, and invoices.
  • Session & Activity Data (Kite & Wing Alliance): Kite sessions logged (spot, date, duration, conditions), XP earned, AllianceScore data, competition entries, and league participation.
  • Community Content: Spot journals, forum posts, comments, reviews, check-ins, photos, videos, and messages exchanged through the Services.
  • Communications: Emails, support tickets, feedback forms, survey responses, and chat messages you send to us.

2.2 Information Collected Automatically

  • Device & Browser Data: IP address, device type, operating system, browser type and version, screen resolution, and unique device identifiers.
  • Usage Data: Pages visited, features used, clickstream data, session duration, referring URLs, interaction patterns, and feature engagement metrics.
  • Location Data: Approximate location derived from IP address. Precise GPS location only when you explicitly enable location services (e.g., for spot check-ins, session logging, or map features).
  • Cookies & Tracking Technologies: See our Cookie Policy for full details. We use Plausible Analytics for privacy-friendly usage analytics.

2.3 Information from Third Parties

  • Session Tracking Devices: Data from connected WOO or Surfr sensors (jump height, airtime, session performance) when you choose to link these services.
  • Weather & Conditions Data: Wind speed, direction, temperature, and forecasts from Open-Meteo, associated with sessions and spot data.
  • Payment Processor: Transaction confirmations and fraud detection signals from Stripe.
  • Authentication Providers: If you sign in via Google or Apple, we receive your name, email, and profile photo as permitted by your settings with those providers.
  • Mapbox: Location and map-related data for spot mapping and visualization.

4. How We Share Your Information

We share personal information only in the following circumstances:

4.1 With Other Users (Based on Your Settings)

  • Your public profile (name, photo, home spot, skill level) is visible to other Services users by default on profile pages.
  • Session logs, check-ins, and AllianceScore are visible on leaderboards and spot pages unless you set them to private.
  • Reviews and forum posts are public by default.
  • You can adjust visibility settings in Account Settings → Privacy at any time.

4.2 With Schools & Instructors (AllyOS)

  • When you book a lesson through AllyOS, the school and assigned instructor receive your name, contact information, skill level, and booking details.
  • Schools can view student milestone progress for students enrolled in their courses.
  • Payment information is shared with schools only in aggregate (not individual card data).

7. Your Rights

7.1 Global Rights (All Users)

All users, regardless of jurisdiction, have the following baseline rights:

  • Access: Request a copy of all personal data we hold about you in a portable, machine-readable format.
  • Correction: Request correction of inaccurate, incomplete, or outdated personal data.
  • Deletion: Request deletion of your account and associated personal data (subject to legal retention obligations).
  • Data Portability: Request your data in a portable format (JSON, CSV export available in Account Settings).

9. Security

We implement comprehensive technical and organizational measures to protect your data:

Technical Security: - Encryption in transit using TLS 1.2+ (all data moving between your device and our servers) - Encryption at rest using AES-256 (all data stored in Supabase) - Row-level security (RLS) policies in our database ensuring multi-tenant data isolation - Secure password hashing using bcrypt with salt - Two-factor authentication (2FA) available for all accounts - API authentication using secure tokens with expiration and rotation

Organizational Security: - Regular security audits by qualified third parties - Dependency vulnerability scanning and automated patching - Role-based access controls (RBAC) for internal team members (principle of least privilege) - Data access logging and monitoring - Incident response procedures and breach notification protocols - Data processing agreements with all service providers - Regular staff training on data protection and information security

Limitations: No system is 100% secure. We cannot guarantee absolute security despite our best efforts. If we become aware of a data breach that affects your personal data, we will:

  • Notify you without undue delay (within 72 hours under GDPR, as soon as reasonably possible under other frameworks)
  • Notify relevant supervisory authorities as required by applicable law
  • Take immediate steps to mitigate the impact and prevent further unauthorized access
  • Document the breach and our response measures
